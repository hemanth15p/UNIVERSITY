Takshashila University




Takshashila University was an ancient centre of learning in India, located in present-day Pakistan. It was founded around the 5th century BCE and flourished until the 5th century CE. It attracted students and scholars from various regions and backgrounds, such as India, Greece, Persia, and China. It offered courses in religious and secular subjects, such as Vedas, grammar, medicine, law, astronomy, and philosophy. It was also a hub of cultural and intellectual exchange, where ideas from different traditions and schools of thought were debated and developed. Some of the famous alumni of Takshashila University include Chanakya, Panini, Charaka, and Jivaka.

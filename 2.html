Chandragupta




Chandragupta Maurya was the founder of the Mauryan Empire, the first empire to unify most of India under one administration. He reigned from 320 BCE to 298 BCE. He was a student of Chanakya, a renowned teacher and strategist who wrote the Arthashastra, a treatise on statecraft, economics, and military tactics. Chandragupta defeated the Nanda dynasty and the Greek satraps in India, and expanded his empire to the borders of Persia. He later embraced Jainism and renounced his throne, fasting to death at Shravanbelagola.

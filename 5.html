Aristotle




Aristotle was a Greek philosopher and scientist who lived from 384 to 322 BCE. He was a student of Plato and a teacher of Alexander the Great. He wrote many books on various subjects, such as logic, physics, biology, ethics, politics, and poetry. He is considered one of the most influential thinkers in the history of Western civilization. He founded the Lyceum, a school and research center in Athens, where he developed his own philosophical system, known as Aristotelianism. He also made important contributions to the development of scientific methods and empirical observation
